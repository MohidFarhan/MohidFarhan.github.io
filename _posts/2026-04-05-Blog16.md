## Introduction

In our previous execution, we ran the Inert Doublet Model (IDM) through the BSM Viability Checklist, which is a gauntlet of theoretical, precision, collider, and cosmological constraints designed to kill theories unviable theoreies. The IDM survived, albeit wounded, its parameter space carved into a narrow wedge of existence by the T-parameter, invisible Higgs decays, and relic density constraints.

But the IDM is not the only heir to the inert throne. Today, we examine the **Inert Doublet + Singlet Model (IDSM)**, which is an extension that adds a complex scalar singlet to the inert sector, enriching the Higgs portal and opening new annihilation channels while preserving the $$Z_2$$ dark symmetry [^33^].

And then there is the titan: the **Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM)**. For decades, it was the paradigm of beyond-the-Standard-Model physics, promising gauge coupling unification, a dark matter candidate in the neutralino, and a solution to the hierarchy problem. But the LHC has been unkind to supersymmetry, pushing mass limits into the multi-TeV regime and forcing the MSSM into ever-shrinking corners of parameter space [^26^][^31^].

We shall subject both to the same four gates of the gauntlet: Theoretical Consistency, Electroweak Precision, Collider Survival, and Dark Matter Viability. And finally, we shall turn to the hidden blades, which include but are not limited to flavor physics, electric dipole moments, and Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, that execute models in the shadows.

Let the second round of executions begin.

## Theoretical Consistency: When the Math Fights Back

### The IDSM: A Singlet's Burden

The IDSM extends the IDM scalar potential with a complex singlet $$S$$, charged under a softly-broken $$U(1)$$ symmetry but even under the $$Z_2$$ that protects dark matter [^33^]. The potential gains new terms:

$$
V \supset m_S^2 |S|^2 + \lambda_S |S|^4 + \lambda_{hs} |H^\dagger H| |S|^2 + \lambda_{hS} |H^\dagger H| (S^2 + S^{\dagger 2})
$$

where $$H$$ denotes the SM Higgs doublet and the inert doublet $$\Phi$$ remains $$Z_2$$-odd. The singlet mixes with the SM Higgs through the portal coupling $$\lambda_{hs}$$, modifying the $$125$$ GeV Higgs signal strengths and introducing new decay channels [^33^].

The beta functions for the quartic couplings now include singlet contributions. At one-loop, the running of the SM Higgs coupling $$\lambda_1$$ receives additional positive contributions:

$$
\beta_{\lambda_1} = \beta_{\lambda_1}^{\text{SM}} + \frac{1}{16\pi^2} (4\lambda_{hs}^2 + 8\lambda_{hS}^2)
$$

while the singlet self-coupling $$\lambda_S$$ and portal couplings $$\lambda_{hs}, \lambda_{hS}$$ evolve according to coupled RGEs that must be checked for Landau poles [^20^]. Unlike the minimal IDM, the IDSM can hit perturbativity limits earlier due to the additional scalar degrees of freedom, with cutoff scales often appearing around $$10^7$$ GeV for portal couplings $$\lambda_{hs} \gtrsim 1$$ [^12^].

Vacuum stability demands that the potential remain bounded from below in all field directions. The singlet introduces new dangerous directions where $$\lambda_S < 0$$ or where the combined Higgs-singlet mass matrix develops negative eigenvalues. The stability conditions generalize to:

$$
\lambda_1 > 0, \quad \lambda_S > 0, \quad \lambda_{hs} + \sqrt{\lambda_1 \lambda_S} > 0
$$

and similar constraints involving the inert sector couplings. The singlet vacuum expectation value must also not trigger spontaneous breaking of the $$Z_2$$ symmetry that protects the dark matter candidate [^33^].

### The MSSM: Supersymmetry's High-Stakes Gamble

The MSSM introduces superpartners for every SM particle: squarks, sleptons, gauginos, and higgsinos. The scalar potential is not written by hand but enforced by supersymmetry, containing $$D$$-terms and $$F$$-terms that automatically preserve vacuum stability at tree-level. However, supersymmetry breaking introduces soft terms, masses $$m_0$$ for scalars, $$M_{1/2}$$ for gauginos, and trilinear $$A$$-terms, that can destabilize the vacuum if pushed too far [^16^].

The gravest danger for the MSSM is **Charge and Color Breaking (CCB)**. The scalar potential contains directions in field space where stop squarks $$\tilde{t}$$, bottom squarks $$\tilde{b}$$, or staus $$\tilde{\tau}$$ acquire vacuum expectation values, breaking electromagnetic or $$SU(3)_C$$ symmetry. The traditional CCB bound requires:

$$
|A_t|^2 \lesssim 6 m_{\tilde{t}_1} m_{\tilde{t}_2}
$$

for the stop trilinear coupling $$A_t$$, ensuring that the physical vacuum with unbroken gauge symmetries is the global minimum [^17^][^18^]. Recent analyses show that for light stops $$\lesssim 1$$ TeV and $$\mu \lesssim 500$$ GeV, the stability condition becomes even more restrictive, carving away parameter space that maximizes the Higgs mass [^17^].

The MSSM beta functions enjoy a remarkable property: gauge coupling unification. The RGEs for the $$SU(3)_C$$, $$SU(2)_L$$, and $$U(1)_Y$$ couplings converge to a common value around $$10^{16}$$ GeV, suggesting the MSSM remains perturbative up to the GUT scale [^11^]. However, this is not guaranteed as large $$\tan\beta$$ or substantial $$A$$-terms can drive the stop coupling to a Landau pole below the Planck scale.

Anomaly cancellation in the MSSM is automatic because superpartners come in complete multiplets with opposite statistics, ensuring that gauge anomalies from the Standard Model fermions cancel against those from their superpartners [^15^].

## Electroweak Precision Tests: The Oblique Guillotine

Both models must confront the Peskin-Takeuchi parameters $$S$$, $$T$$, and $$U$$, which constrain new physics contributions to electroweak vacuum polarization amplitudes [^2^].

In the **IDSM**, the singlet scalar does not couple directly to gauge bosons (being a gauge singlet), so it contributes to oblique parameters only at loop-level through mixing with the Higgs. The dominant constraints come from the inert doublet sector, identical to the IDM: the mass splitting between the charged scalar $$H^\pm$$ and the dark matter candidate $$H^0$$ is constrained by $$\Delta T \lesssim 0.12$$, requiring near-degenerate masses $$\Delta m \lesssim 50$$ GeV [^33^].

The **MSSM** faces a more complex assault. The charginos and neutralinos contribute to $$S$$ and $$T$$ through loops involving gauge bosons and Higgs bosons. The chargino mass matrix depends on $$M_2$$, $$\mu$$, and $$\tan\beta$$:

$$
M_{\tilde{\chi}^\pm} = \begin{pmatrix} M_2 & \sqrt{2} m_W \sin\beta \\ \sqrt{2} m_W \cos\beta & \mu \end{pmatrix}
$$

and the neutralino mass matrix involves $$M_1$$, $$M_2$$, $$\mu$$, and the $$Z$$ boson mass [^25^][^29^]. For naturalness, one desires light higgsinos ($$\mu \sim 100-300$$ GeV), but electroweak precision tests prefer degenerate chargino-neutralino spectra or heavy spectra where the contributions decouple. LEP constraints require chargino masses $$m_{\tilde{\chi}_1^\pm} \gtrsim 103.5$$ GeV [^32^], while precision fits favor mass splittings that do not disturb the $$T$$ parameter excessively.

## Collider Constraints: The LHC's Reckoning

### IDSM: Higgs Signatures and Missing Energy

The IDSM inherits all collider constraints from the IDM, such as charged scalars decaying to $$W^\pm H$$, mono-$$X$$ searches, and invisible Higgs decays $$h \to HH$$, but adds the complication of singlet mixing. The singlet-modified Higgs couplings deviate from SM predictions, constrained by LHC signal strength measurements to be within $$10-20\%$$ of SM values [^33^].

The mono-$$X$$ corridor becomes more complex. The dark matter candidate $$H^0$$ can now annihilate through the singlet-enhanced Higgs portal, allowing for smaller $$\lambda_{345}$$ (the IDM portal coupling) while maintaining the correct relic density. This relaxes LHC mono-jet constraints but introduces tension with direct detection experiments [^33^].

### MSSM: The Gluino Massacre

The LHC Run 2 and Run 3 have been devastating for the MSSM. Searches for strong production of gluinos and squarks have pushed limits into the multi-TeV regime:

- **Gluinos**: excluded up to $$2.2-2.4$$ TeV for massless neutralinos, and up to $$\sim 2$$ TeV even for heavy neutralinos [^26^][^28^].
- **First/second generation squarks**: excluded up to $$1.2-1.7$$ TeV depending on decay modes [^28^][^31^].
- **Third generation stops**: excluded up to $$1.2$$ TeV in naturalness-favored scenarios [^26^].
- **Electroweakinos**: chargino and neutralino masses excluded up to $$\sim 1$$ TeV in compressed or wino-dominated scenarios [^26^][^31^].

The "natural" MSSM, where stops and gluinos live near the TeV scale to cancel quadratic divergences in the Higgs mass, has been largely decimated. Surviving scenarios feature:
- **Compressed spectra**: where mass splittings between charginos/neutralinos are small, suppressing lepton $$p_T$$ and evading detection [^28^].
- **R-parity violation**: though this destroys the dark matter candidate.
- **Gravitino LSP**: where the neutralino is no longer stable, altering collider signatures and BBN predictions [^3^][^13^].

## Dark Matter Specifics: The Cosmic Accounting

### IDSM: Resonance Engineering

The IDSM dark matter candidate remains the lightest $$Z_2$$-odd neutral scalar from the inert doublet, but its cosmology is enriched by the singlet-modified Higgs sector. The singlet mixes with the Higgs, creating additional resonance channels for annihilation [^33^]:

$$
\Omega h^2 \approx \frac{1.07 \times 10^9 \text{ GeV}^{-1}}{g_*^{1/2} M_{\text{Pl}} \langle \sigma v \rangle}
$$

The relic density calculation must now include annihilation through the singlet-like Higgs eigenstates, allowing for destructive interference between the doublet and singlet contributions to the Higgs portal coupling. This can suppress $$\langle \sigma v \rangle$$ near resonances at $$m_H \approx m_{h_1,S}/2$$, opening viable parameter space where the IDM would overclose the universe [^33^].

Direct detection rates remain suppressed by the small portal coupling required to avoid invisible Higgs decays, with spin-independent cross sections typically $$\sigma_{\text{SI}} \sim 10^{-11}-10^{-13}$$ pb, well below current XENON-nT and LZ limits but approaching the neutrino fog [^1^][^33^].

### MSSM: The Neutralino's Last Stand

The MSSM offers several dark matter candidates, but the canonical choice is the lightest neutralino $$\tilde{\chi}_1^0$$ which is a mixture of bino ($$U(1)_Y$$ gaugino), wino ($$SU(2)_L$$ gaugino), and higgsino [^7^]. Its composition is determined by the parameters $$M_1$$, $$M_2$$, $$\mu$$, and $$\tan\beta$$:

- **Bino-like**: Pure bino LSPs overproduce dark matter unless co-annihilation channels with sleptons or stops are available [^3^][^5^].
- **Wino-like**: Winos annihilate too efficiently via $$SU(2)$$ gauge interactions, producing under-abundance unless $$m_{\tilde{w}} \gtrsim 3$$ TeV [^1^].
- **Higgsino-like**: Higgsinos annihilate through gauge interactions with cross sections $$\langle \sigma v \rangle \sim 10^{-24}$$ cm$$^3$$/s, requiring masses $$\sim 1$$ TeV to match the relic density [^1^].
- **Mixed**: The "well-tempered" neutralino (part bino, part higgsino) can achieve the thermal relic density $$0.120 \pm 0.001$$ for masses $$100-500$$ GeV, but LHC constraints on chargino masses ($$m_{\tilde{\chi}_1^\pm} \sim \mu$$) often exclude these regions [^3^][^31^].

Direct detection constraints are severe for heavy neutralinos with significant higgsino components, as they couple to nuclei through $$Z$$ and Higgs exchange. The spin-independent cross sections for bino-higgsino mixtures can exceed $$10^{-46}$$ cm$$^2$$, excluded by LUX, PandaX-4T, and XENON-nT for masses above $$\sim 50$$ GeV [^3^][^5^].

## The Hidden Blades: Execution in the Shadows

### Flavor Physics and $$(g-2)_\mu$$

The **IDSM** is flavor-safe because the $$Z_2$$ symmetry prevents tree-level couplings to fermions. However, it cannot explain the muon $$g-2$$ anomaly, as contributions appear only at two-loop order via Barr-Zee diagrams, yielding $$\Delta a_\mu \sim 10^{-11}$$, far below the observed $$251 \times 10^{-11}$$ deviation [^1^].

The **MSSM** can explain $$(g-2)_\mu$$ through loops involving smuons, neutralinos, and charginos, but only if smuons are light ($$\sim 100-200$$ GeV) and $$\tan\beta$$ is large. Such light sleptons are increasingly excluded by LHC searches for dilepton plus MET signatures [^3^][^14^].

Electric Dipole Moments (EDMs) pose existential threats to the MSSM with complex phases. The electron EDM is constrained to $$d_e \leq 1.1 \times 10^{-29}$$ e·cm by ACME. If the MSSM contains CP-violating phases in $$M_1$$, $$M_2$$, or $$\mu$$, one-loop contributions to EDMs can exceed this bound by orders of magnitude [^14^]. Stringent constraints require phases $$\lesssim 10^{-5}$$ radians, challenging the "naturalness" of CP violation in supersymmetric models.

### Big Bang Nucleosynthesis and Proton Decay

In **gravitino dark matter** scenarios, where the gravitino is the LSP and the neutralino is unstable, the MSSM faces BBN constraints. The neutralino decays into gravitinos and SM particles, injecting electromagnetic and hadronic energy during nucleosynthesis ($$t \sim 1-1000$$ seconds). This can destroy the agreement with helium-4 and deuterium abundances unless the reheating temperature $$T_R \lesssim 10^7-10^8$$ GeV and the neutralino lifetime $$\tau \lesssim 3 \times 10^4$$ seconds [^13^][^19^].

Furthermore, negatively charged long-lived particles (like staus) form bound states with nuclei during BBN, catalyzing the production of $$^6\text{Li}$$ and destroying $$^7\text{Li}$$ unless the stau lifetime is carefully tuned [^13^][^19^].

Unlike GUTs, the MSSM with R-parity conservation does not predict proton decay at tree-level. However, dimension-5 operators mediated by squarks can induce proton decay, requiring sfermion masses $$\gtrsim 10^{15}$$ GeV or specific flavor structures to avoid Super-Kamiokande limits [^15^].

## Conclusion

We have subjected the IDSM and MSSM to the gauntlet. The IDSM survives as a wounded but viable extension of the IDM, its singlet offering parametric freedom at the cost of UV incompleteness. The MSSM, once the emperor of BSM physics, has been dethroned by the LHC, its naturalness decapitated by multi-TeV gluino limits, its dark matter candidate hunted by xenon detectors, its flavor sector constrained by EDMs.

Yet both models cling to existence in narrow, tuned regions of parameter space. They are "not dead yet", but they are certainly bleeding.

The gauntlet does not care for elegance. It cares only for numbers: cross sections, branching ratios, relic densities, and decay widths. While the beautiful ideas are cheap, surviving the gauntlet isn't.
